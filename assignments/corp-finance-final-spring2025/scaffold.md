## Remember (definitions & vocabulary)

- [ ] `gap` — **Conglomerate merger**: Combination of firms in unrelated industries; theoretical cash-flow diversification but limited value since investors can diversify themselves. → **Q1**
- [ ] `gap` — **Hostile takeover**: Acquirer bypasses target's board and appeals directly to shareholders (tender offer / proxy fight). → **Q2**
- [ ] `gap` — **Asset purchase vs. stock purchase**: Asset → buy specific assets/liabilities, basis step-up, no inherited liabilities/NOLs, no shareholder vote required for target. Stock → buy entire entity, inherit everything. → **Q3, Q14**
- [ ] `gap` — **Poison pill**: Takeover defense; issues discounted shares to existing shareholders (ex-bidder) when a raider crosses ownership threshold. → **Q4**
- [ ] `gap` — **Stock vs. cash consideration**: Stock lets acquirer share valuation risk with target (target keeps upside/downside exposure); cash transfers all risk to acquirer. → **Q5**
- [ ] `gap` — **Ambiguity aversion**: Investors demand extra premium for situations with unknown probability distributions (not just known risk). → **Q6**
- [ ] `gap` — **Short-selling constraints**: Restrictions (locate/borrow, margin, regulatory) that **impede** arbitrage — they are a *limit to* arbitrage, not an aid. → **Q7**
- [ ] `gap` — **MM Proposition 1**: In perfect capital markets, total firm value is **invariant** to capital structure. → **Q8**
- [ ] `gap` — **MM Proposition 2**: r_E = r_U + (D/E)(r_U − r_D). Leverage raises equity cost linearly with D/E. WACC is invariant without taxes. → **Q17, P3(h)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Counterparty risk & marking to market**: Futures exchange marks positions daily; P&L settled each day → reduces default exposure. → **Q9**
- [ ] `gap` — **In-the-money vs. profitable**: ITM = positive intrinsic value; profitable = intrinsic value > premium paid. The two aren't the same. → **Q10**
- [ ] `gap` — **FCFF = EBIT(1−τ) + D&A − CapEx − ΔNWC**: Free cash flow available to all capital providers. → **Q11, Q12, P1**
- [ ] `gap` — **P/E ratio drivers (Gordon)**: P/E ∝ payout / (r − g). Higher g, lower r → higher P/E. → **Q13**
- [ ] `gap` — **EMH**: Prices reflect available info and move only on surprises; technical analysis unprofitable on average; even skilled investors can't consistently beat risk-adjusted benchmarks. → **Q16**
- [ ] `gap` — **Overreaction**: Prices move too much in response to news, later reversing (CUBA fund example). → **Q15**
- [ ] `gap` — **Forward/futures contracts — common use**: Predominantly FX hedging (cross-border revenue/expense). → **Q18**
- [ ] `gap` — **Payoffs — long put**: Max(K−S, 0); gains if S falls. Long call: Max(S−K, 0); gains if S rises. Long forward/futures: S−F; gains if S rises. → **Q19, P4(b,c)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Call options for employees**: Align incentives with equity upside, conserve cash, retention via vesting — but they **encourage** volatility/risk-taking (options gain from vol). → **Q20**
- [ ] `gap` — **CAPM**: r_E = r_f + β × MRP. → **P1(a)**
- [ ] `gap` — **WACC with tax**: WACC = (E/V)·r_E + (D/V)·r_D·(1−τ). → **P1(b), P3(h)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Terminal value (growing perpetuity)**: TV = FCFF × (1+g)/(r−g); discount to today. → **P1(e)**
- [ ] `gap` — **P/E ratio**: Share price / EPS (or Market cap / Net income). → **P2(a)**
- [ ] `gap` — **P/B ratio**: Share price / Book value per share (or Market cap / Book equity). → **P2(a)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Takeover premium**: (Offer − Standalone value) / Standalone value. → **P2(d)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Synergy division**: Synergies split between acquirer (offer < standalone + synergy) and target (premium above standalone). → **P2(e)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Enterprise value**: EV = Equity + Debt − Cash. → **P3(a)**
- [ ] `gap` — **PV of tax shield (permanent debt)**: τ_c × D (perpetual debt discounted at r_D). → **P3(d)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Trade-off theory of capital structure**: V_L = V_U + PV(tax shields) − PV(distress costs) − other side effects. → **P3(e,f,g)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Financial distress costs**: Direct (legal, bankruptcy fees) + indirect (lost customers/suppliers/employees, fire-sales). → **P3(f)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Risk-shifting (asset substitution)**: Levered equity holders prefer riskier projects (option-like payoff); destroys value for bondholders. → **P3(f)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Money-to-burn / free-cash-flow problem**: Excess cash in managers' hands → wasteful projects; debt disciplines by forcing payouts. → **P3(f)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Risk aversion by management**: Managers under-lever relative to optimum because of career/undiversified-wealth concerns. → **P3(f)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Cost of carry (no-arbitrage futures)**: F = S × (1 + r_f + storage − convenience)^T. → **P4(a)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Put-call parity**: C − P = S − K/(1+r)^T (or S − PV(K) for a non-dividend-paying asset). On a futures underlying: C − P = (F − K)/(1+r)^T. → **P4(d,e,f)**
- [ ] `gap` — **Limits to arbitrage**: Capital constraints, short-sale constraints, noise-trader risk, fundamental risk, execution costs. → **P4(g)**

## Understand (mechanics & judgments)

- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why conglomerate diversification has limited value**: Shareholders can diversify cheaper than firms can by buying multiple stocks — so firm-level diversification alone rarely creates value unless paired with real synergies. → **Q1**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why stock consideration shares overvaluation risk**: If acquirer stock is overvalued, target shareholders bear part of the correction. Cash transfers all overvaluation risk to the acquirer. → **Q5**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why ambiguity aversion raises cost of capital**: Investors penalize uncertain projects beyond the CAPM-implied discount, demanding extra return. → **Q6**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why short-selling constraints hurt efficiency**: They prevent negative information from being incorporated into prices → overpricing can persist. → **Q7**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why "MM1 implies firm value is invariant" (T/F nuance)**: MM1 says firm value doesn't depend on capital structure in perfect markets — NOT that value "can increase or decrease depending on equity." → **Q8**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why ITM options aren't always profitable to exercise**: You paid a premium up front; exercise yields intrinsic value, but net profit = intrinsic − premium. Can be ITM and still net losing. → **Q10**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why depreciation raises FCFF**: FCFF = EBIT(1−τ) + D&A − CapEx − ΔNWC. D&A reduces EBIT but is added back (non-cash) and also creates tax-shield value via lower taxes. → **Q11**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why sunk consulting fees aren't in FCFF**: Only **incremental** post-decision cash flows matter. Already-paid fees are sunk. → **Q12**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why lower equity beta raises P/E**: Lower β → lower r → higher P/E given the same earnings and growth. → **Q13**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why asset purchases do not inherit NOLs**: Tax basis steps up in an asset deal; NOLs belong to the selling entity's shell, not the purchased assets. → **Q14**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why CUBA surge is "overreaction"**: Price moved on news that didn't affect fundamentals (fund had no Cuba exposure); sentiment-driven over-movement. → **Q15**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why EMH does NOT imply skilled investors can consistently outperform**: Exactly the opposite — EMH claims consistent outperformance is essentially impossible. → **Q16**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why MM2's cost of equity rises with leverage**: More senior debt → residual equity is riskier → investors demand higher r_E. Total WACC unchanged (no taxes). → **Q17, P3(h)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why forwards dominate FX hedging use**: Firms operate across currencies daily; FX exposure is pervasive and easily hedged with forwards. → **Q18**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why long put (only) among listed options gains when spot falls**: Only instrument with payoff increasing as S decreases (of the listed). → **Q19**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why options discourage — or encourage — risk-taking**: Options gain asymmetrically from volatility (convex payoff), so they can *encourage* excessive risk-taking, not discourage. → **Q20**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why a real option to abandon increases NPV**: Optionality to cut losses truncates the left tail of returns → expected value ↑. → **P1(g)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why P/B is often better than P/E for early-stage biotech**: Near-zero or negative earnings make P/E unstable; P/B anchors to tangible/accounting equity. → **P2(c)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why Medtronic dropping and Lumon rising implies overpayment**: Deal-announcement stock moves capture market's estimate of how synergies are divided. Acquirer drop → market thinks acquirer is overpaying relative to its share of synergies. → **P2(f)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why MM1 post-recap market cap = pre-recap EV − debt issued**: In perfect markets, total EV unchanged. Equity holders get cash back equal to debt raised. → **P3(b)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why the "side effect" in Globex is negative**: Actual EV − perfect-market EV − PV(tax shield) is negative → something off-trade-off is destroying value (distress, risk-shifting, etc.). → **P3(e,f)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why Scorpio's "cheap debt lowers WACC" logic is flawed in perfect markets**: Adding debt raises r_E in exact proportion — WACC unchanged. The savings don't materialize without tax/other frictions. → **P3(h)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why a seller short futures AND long puts to hedge a decline**: Both have payoff that rises as S falls; the seller locks in a floor (or at-cost-of-premium with puts). → **P4(b)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why long call + short put replicates long forward**: Put-call parity: (C − P) at strike K mimics a forward at K; payoff identical at expiry. → **P4(d)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why violating put-call parity creates arbitrage**: If C − P ≠ PV(S − K), construct synthetic position (buy cheap, sell expensive) to lock in risk-free profit. → **P4(f)**

## Analyze (higher-order — trade-offs, cross-statement consistency)

- [ ] `not assessed` — **Trade-off theory integration**: Firm value = V_U + PV(TS) − PV(distress) ± other side effects (risk-shifting, agency, signaling). Use residual side-effect sign to infer which force dominates. → **P3(e,f,g)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Market reaction as signal about deal quality**: Acquirer drop at announcement → market thinks deal NPV to acquirer is negative (overpaying / poor synergy estimates). Target rise → market believes takeover premium is real. → **P2(f)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **MM Prop 2 intuition — "cheap debt" fallacy**: Cost of equity scales exactly with leverage under MM, keeping WACC constant. Debt isn't "cheap" net of the increased equity risk it creates. → **P3(h)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Put-call parity as arbitrage anchor**: Treat C − P − PV(F − K) = 0 as an equilibrium; any violation → explicit trade that locks risk-free profit. → **P4(d,e,f)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Real options as NPV-adjustment**: Standard DCF understates project value when managers have flexibility (abandon, expand, delay). Abandon = put option on continuation CFs. → **P1(g)**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why P/B may differ from P/E implied values**: Multiples on different anchors diverge when companies are at different lifecycle stages; choice matters most for early-stage/intangible-heavy firms. → **P2(c)**
