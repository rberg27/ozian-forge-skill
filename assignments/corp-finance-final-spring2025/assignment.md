## Part III — Multi-Part Problems (77 points total)

### Problem 1 — Ditta Project Valuation (20 points)

Ditta is launching a **miso-based candy bar**. Valuation inputs:
- Initial investment: $100M at t=0
- EBIT: $5M (yr 1), $8M (yr 2)
- Depreciation: $3M (yr 1 and 2)
- Yr 3+: FCFF grows at 2% in perpetuity
- Risk-free = 4%, MRP = 7%, equity beta = 2
- D/(D+E) = 40%, cost of debt = 5%, marginal tax = 20%, effective tax = 7%

- **(a, 3 pt):** Cost of equity?
- **(b, 3 pt):** WACC?
- **(c, 4 pt):** FCFF years 0–2?
- **(d, 3 pt):** NPV of FCFF years 0–2?
- **(e, 3 pt):** NPV of terminal value?
- **(f, 2 pt):** NPV of project; proceed?
- **(g, 2 pt):** Would a real option to abandon after yr 2 increase NPV?

### Problem 2 — Lumon M&A (15 points)

Medtronic is considering acquiring Lumon Industries (biotech, neuro-modulation). $30M in expected synergies.

| | NeuroNova | Synaptech | Lumon |
|---|---|---|---|
| Net income | $8M | $6M | proj. $4M |
| Book equity | $24M | $20M | proj. $15M |
| Share price | $30 | $36 | — |
| Shares | 4M | 3M | — |

- **(a, 3 pt):** Average P/E and P/B across comparables?
- **(b, 2 pt):** Implied Lumon equity value under each multiple?
- **(c, 2 pt):** Which multiple is more appropriate for Lumon? Why?
- **(d, 3 pt):** Takeover premium if Medtronic offers $80M cash?
- **(e, 2 pt):** Division of synergies under $80M cash offer?
- **(f, 3 pt):** Medtronic −4%, Lumon +20% post-announcement. Unusual? What does it imply about investor view?

### Problem 3 — Recapitalization at Globex (20 points)

Globex: 100% equity, 200M shares at $15, no cash. Issues $1.2B debt to repurchase shares. r_D=6%, r_E=13%, τ=35%. Post-recap equity cap = $2.5B.

- **(a, 2 pt):** EV **before** the recapitalization?
- **(b, 2 pt):** With perfect capital markets, what would post-recap market cap and EV be?
- **(c, 2 pt):** Actual post-recap EV?
- **(d, 3 pt):** PV of tax shield (permanent debt)?
- **(e, 3 pt):** PV of other financial side effects (from market reaction)?
- **(f, 3 pt):** Which trade-off best explains the side effects? (financial distress / risk-shifting / risk aversion by mgmt / money-to-burn)
- **(g, 2 pt):** How does that trade-off shape leverage–value relationship?
- **(h, 3 pt):** Is Scorpio's "keep adding debt to lower WACC" logic flawed even under perfect markets?

### Problem 4 — Hedging Corn (22 points)

Prairie Valley Farms will sell 100,000 bushels of corn in 6 months. Spot = $5.00/bushel, rf = 5.5%, storage cost = 10.60% (ignore for options).

- **(a, 3 pt):** 6-month futures price of corn?
- **(b, 2 pt):** To hedge against a **decline**, should Prairie go long or short (i) futures, (ii) puts?
- **(c, 6 pt):** Payoff diagrams for each of the two hedge strategies (put strike = futures price).
- **(d, 3 pt):** Portfolio of call + put that replicates the futures hedge?
- **(e, 3 pt):** Put price = $1.05, same strike. Call price implied by put-call parity?
- **(f, 3 pt):** If the call trades at $0.65, is there an arbitrage? What trades?
- **(g, 2 pt):** Two examples of real-world limits to arbitrage.
