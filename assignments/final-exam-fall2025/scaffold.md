## Remember (definitions & vocabulary — tested as "identify" or "define")

- [x] `understood` — **Effective interest method**: Interest expense = beginning book value × effective (market) rate at issuance. → **I.2, I.6**
- [x] `gap` — **Par / discount / premium at issuance**: Coupon = market → par; coupon < market → discount; coupon > market → premium. → **I.3**
- [x] `understood` — **Bond price–yield inverse relationship**: Market rate up → bond price down, and vice versa. → **I.4A**
- [x] `understood` — **Book value vs. fair value of debt**: Book = amortized cost (PV at original effective rate); FV = PV at current market rate. → **I.4B**
- [x] `gap` — **Purchase obligations**: Executory contracts — goods/services not yet received; disclosed in footnotes but not on the balance sheet. → **I.7**
- [x] `gap` — **Permanent vs. temporary differences**: Permanent = never reverse (affect ETR, no DTA/DTL); temporary = reverse in future (create DTA/DTL). → **II.3, II.4, II.6, II.7**
- [x] `gap` — **Valuation allowance**: Contra-DTA reflecting the portion of DTAs not "more likely than not" to be realized. Increasing VA → tax expense up → NI down. → **II.5**
- [x] `gap` — **NOL carryforward**: Prior-year tax losses that offset future taxable income. DTA = NOL × statutory rate. → **II.4**
- [x] `gap` — **Grant-date fair value method**: Stock-option comp expense is fixed at grant (not remeasured) and recognized over the vesting period. → **III.2, III.3**
- [x] `gap` — **Dividends declared vs. paid**: Declaration creates the liability and reduces RE; payment reduces cash and the liability. → **III.4** 
- [x] `understood` — **Dilutive securities**: Securities that, if converted/exercised, increase shares outstanding and reduce EPS (options, RSUs, PSUs, convertibles). → **III.5**
- [x] `gap` — **Stock dividend (small, <25%)**: Capitalize FV into common stock/APIC from RE; no cash, no total-equity change, but share count grows. → **III.6**
- [x] `gap` — **Treasury stock**: Previously issued shares repurchased — contra-equity at cost; reduces shares outstanding, not shares issued. → **III.7, III.8**
- [x] `gap` — **Avoidable vs. unavoidable costs**: Only avoidable costs + opportunity cost differ between alternatives and belong in a make-buy / keep-drop decision. → **IV.1**
- [x] `gap` — **Contribution margin**: Price − variable cost (per unit or in total); covers fixed costs first, then profit. → **IV.2**
- [x] `gap` — **Plantwide (single-driver) OH rate vs. ABC**: Single rate spreads all OH via one driver (usually DLH); ABC uses multiple pools and drivers. → **IV.3A, IV.3B, IV.3D**
- [x] `gap` — **Cost distortion / cross-subsidy**: Single-driver systems over-cost products that consume lots of the driver and under-cost products that consume little — cross-subsidizing through OH. → **IV.3D**
- [x] `understood` — **Transfer price — general rule**: TP = variable cost + opportunity cost of the selling division (= external market price when the division is at capacity). → **V.1**
- [x] `understood` — **Tax-motivated transfer pricing**: In a multi-jurisdiction firm, TP shifts income across tax rates — raising firm profit only if rates differ and the firm can legally set the price. → **V.4**

## Understand (mechanics & judgments)

- [x] `understood` — **Why cash payment is constant for bonds but not always for mortgages**: Bonds pay coupon = stated-rate × face (fixed dollar amount each period); mortgages typically pay a constant *total* payment split between interest and principal (principal portion grows). → **I.2**
- [x] `gap` — **Why book value of debt on balance sheet is PV at original effective rate**: Amortized cost locks in the rate at issuance so that interest expense reflects the true cost at that time. Subsequent market moves don't flow through. → **I.2, I.4B**
- [x] `understood` — **How to infer issue price sign (par/discount/premium)**: Compare the coupon rate printed in the indenture to the market rate at issuance (use the issue-date market data if given, else reason from the issue price relative to face). → **I.3**
- [x] `gap` — **Gain/loss on early retirement**: Gain if cash paid < book value; loss if cash paid > book value. FV > book value → loss on retirement. → **I.4B**
- [x] `gap` — **Journal entry at issuance (premium/discount)**: DR Cash (proceeds), CR Bonds Payable (face), DR/CR Discount/Premium plug. → **I.5**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Semi-annual interest JE**: DR Interest Expense (book value × effective rate / 2); CR Cash (face × coupon / 2); DR/CR Discount/Premium amortization plug. → **I.6**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Effective-tax-rate bridge**: Statutory rate + permanent differences + state tax + other items = ETR. Nondeductible items push ETR up. → **II.3**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Gross-up from DTA/DTL to underlying difference**: Underlying book-tax difference = DTA (or DTL) ÷ statutory rate. → **II.4, II.6**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Direction of VA-change effect on NI**: Increase in VA → DR Tax Expense / CR VA → NI down. Decrease does the reverse. → **II.5**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **DTA vs. DTL from depreciation**: Tax D&A > Book D&A → lower taxable income now → DTL (future taxes owed). Book > Tax → DTA. → **II.6**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why comp expense creates a DTA**: Book expenses over vesting period (current NI reduced); tax deduction only at exercise (future). Future tax savings → DTA. → **II.7**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **No remeasurement of option expense after grant**: GAAP fixes grant-date FV; price swings don't alter expense. → **III.3**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Cash-flow effects of options**: No cash flow at grant or recognition. Cash inflow to financing when exercised (at strike). → **III.3**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **What drives diluted < basic EPS**: Inclusion of dilutive potential shares (options in-the-money via treasury-stock method; RSUs/PSUs expected to vest; if-converted debt/preferred). Lower income alone doesn't create dilution. → **III.5**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **10% stock dividend mechanics**: RE decreases by FV of shares issued; Common Stock + APIC increase. No cash. No change to total equity. Shares outstanding +10%. → **III.6**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **ROE response to stock dividend**: Numerator (NI) unchanged; denominator (average equity) unchanged → ROE unchanged → bonus likelihood unchanged. RE lower; total equity unchanged. Stock comp expense unaffected. → **III.6**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Shares issued vs. outstanding**: Outstanding = Issued − Treasury. Parenthetical on balance sheet lists both. → **III.7**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **JE for buyback at cost**: DR Treasury Stock (at cost), CR Cash. → **III.8**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why fixed costs survive make-vs-buy unless stated**: Unless the problem says fixed costs are avoidable (e.g., "avoidable fixed OH"), treat them as sunk. → **IV.1**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Opportunity cost of freeing capacity**: Rent income from freed capacity is an inflow to the BUY option — or equivalently, an opportunity cost of MAKE. → **IV.1**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Breakeven = FC ÷ CM/unit**: Target-profit breakeven = (FC + target profit) ÷ CM/unit. → **IV.2**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Plantwide rate mechanics**: Rate = Total OH ÷ Total DLH. Apply rate × DLH per unit to each product. → **IV.3A**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **ABC rate mechanics**: Rate_per_driver = Pool $ ÷ Total driver volume. Unit cost = Σ(driver usage × rate) + direct costs. → **IV.3B, IV.3C**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Why ABC reallocates**: Products that consume disproportionate non-DL resources (setups, handling, packaging) were undercharged by plantwide DL; ABC pulls cost back to them. → **IV.3D**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Transfer price when external market exists**: At capacity → TP = market price (= VC + opp cost). Excess capacity → TP can drop toward VC. → **V.1**
- [ ] `not assessed` — **Firm-wide profit invariance to TP (before tax)**: Intracompany sales net to zero in consolidation. TP only matters for divisional measurement — and for tax when jurisdictions differ. → **V.4**
