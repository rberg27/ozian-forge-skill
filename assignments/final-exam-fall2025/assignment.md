## Problem I — Long-term Liabilities (27 points)

**Q3 (MC, 2 pt):** The 2.600% Senior Notes due April 15, 2030 — issued at **par, discount, or premium**?

**Q4A (MC, 2 pt):** Given fair value of 2030 Senior Notes = $700M at June 30, 2024 — how did the market interest rate change since issuance? (increased / decreased / no change)

**Q4B (Numeric, 4 pt):** Gain or loss if Estee Lauder retired the 2030 notes on June 30, 2024? Indicate gain/loss/none and amount.

**Q5 (Journal Entry, 5 pt):** Record the journal entry Estee Lauder made **at issuance** of the 5.000% Senior Notes due June 30, 2034 (assume issued on June 30, 2024).

**Q6 (Journal Entry, 6 pt):** Summary journal entry for the **first year of six-months interest payment** on the 2034 Senior Notes.

**Q7 (MC, 2 pt):** Per Note 17, Estee Lauder has purchase obligations of $2,370M as of June 30, 2024. How much is on the Balance Sheet? ($2,370M / $1,463M / $98M / $0)

**Q1 (Make-vs-Buy, 5 pt — UM Company):** 15,000 units at $39/unit (supplier raising 20%). Internal per-unit costs: DM $18, DL $12, VMOH $10, FC $8. If buying, can rent excess capacity for $22,500/yr. Make or buy?

**Q2 (Breakeven, 5 pt — Ibis Food Market):**

**Q3 (Costing Systems, 20 pt — Douglass Company):** 4,000 computer parts + 2,000 cell phone parts. DLH: 2/computer, 0.5/cellphone. DM: $30/$17. DL: $16/$4. Budgeted factory OH = $270,000.

- (C, 8 pt) Total cost per unit under **ABC**.
- (D, 3 pt) Why did cost estimates change from traditional to ABC?

## Problem V — Transfer Pricing (16 points — ABC Miners)

Japan Mining Division (raw emerald): VC £50, FC £100 per carat.
UK Processing Division (polished): VC £150, FC £350 per carat of polished emerald.
External market for raw emerald: 9,000 Yen or £180/carat (FX 50 Yen = £1).
Tax rates: Japan 20%, UK 30%.
Yield: 2 carats raw → 1 carat polished. Polished sells £3,000/carat.

**Q1 (MC, 2 pt):** Appropriate transfer price per raw carat per the model taught in class? (Variable cost / Market price = VC + opportunity cost / Full cost plus markup / Dual)

**Q2 (Numeric, 4 pt):** Transfer price of 1 carat raw emerald at (a) 200% of Full cost, (b) Market Price.

**Q3 (Numeric, 7 pt):** 1,000 raw carats mined → polished → sold. Compute **after-tax income for each division** using **Market Price** as transfer price.

**Q4 (MC, 3 pt):** Market Price vs. 200% of Full Cost — which provides greater **overall firm** profit? (Market / 200% FC / No difference)
