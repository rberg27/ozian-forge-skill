## Effective interest method — 09:00

**Probe:** Can you tell me what the effective interest method is?

**User response:** Interest expense is calculated each period as book value × effective rate at issuance. Cash payment = coupon rate × face value (fixed). Price differs from face value based on market rate vs. coupon rate. Discount bond = price < face value.

## Par / discount / premium at issuance — 09:05

**Probe:** What determines whether a bond is issued at par, discount, or premium?

**User response:** Par = coupon rate equals market rate. Premium = coupon > market (investors want it, pay more). Discount = market > coupon.

**Follow-ups:** None needed.

**Outcome:** understood

## Bond price–yield inverse relationship — 09:10

**Probe:** After a bond is issued, market rates move. Why does that affect the bond's value, and which direction?

**User response:** Rates down → value up; rates up → value down. Correctly distinguished book value (locked at original effective rate) from fair value (moves with market).
