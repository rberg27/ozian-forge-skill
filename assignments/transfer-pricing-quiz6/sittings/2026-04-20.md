## Transfer Price Formula — Remember

**Probe:** Why would a company set a formal price on goods transferred between its own divisions rather than moving them for free?

**User response:** Tax incentive (initially); then after probing: divisions can't account for profit they're responsible for.

**Follow-ups:** What risk does a too-low price create for the selling division? → manager would push to sell externally. What price makes the manager indifferent? → market price. Does market price still apply with idle capacity? → no, floor would be at cost. What's changing between the two cases? → margin (needed prompting) → opportunity cost.

**Outcome:** gap
**Gap detail:** Could not state the formula unprompted; named the endpoints but not the unifying formula; said "margin" instead of "opportunity cost."
**Teaching:** Transfer Price = Variable Cost + Opportunity Cost. Opportunity cost = $0 at idle capacity; opportunity cost = foregone contribution margin at full capacity.
**Verified:** yes

## Variable vs. Fixed Cost — Remember

**Probe:** Why does it matter whether a cost is variable or fixed in a transfer pricing decision?

**User response:** Managers control variable costs, not fixed costs; transfer price should reflect variable cost only.

**Follow-ups:** What goes wrong for the buying division if fixed costs are included? → buying division overpays, gets incentivized to buy externally even when internal is better for the company.

**Outcome:** understood
