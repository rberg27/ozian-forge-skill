## Treasury Stock — Definition — 09:03

**Probe:** Can you tell me what treasury stock is?

**User response:** Said it was "the stock originally issued when a company is incorporated" — then "stock that stays in the company."

**Follow-ups:** Asked what it means for stock to end up back inside the company; asked where the shares came from if the company spent cash to get them; asked user to explain the motivation for buybacks (fewer shares, EPS goes up); user then correctly said shares came from the market.

**Outcome:** gap
**Gap detail:** Initially confused treasury stock with authorized-but-unissued shares; didn't know it requires active repurchase from the market.
**Teaching:** Treasury stock = shares previously issued and outstanding, repurchased for cash. Sits in equity with a debit balance → contra-equity account.
**Verified:** yes

## RSU — Definition — 09:15

**Probe:** Can you tell me what an RSU is?

**User response:** "Stock someone is entitled to buy at a certain price" — described a stock option.

**Follow-ups:** Asked what happens if stock price drops below the set price (user: worthless — value is the spread); asked what the restriction in RSU is (user guessed selling); asked why a company would restrict it (user got to vesting/service period); asked what the employee pays on vesting — user still said "buys at a given price." Taught directly after 5 rounds.

**Outcome:** gap
**Gap detail:** Consistently described RSUs as options (right to buy at set price). Did not know that RSUs are granted for free — no purchase price.
**Teaching:** RSU = shares granted for free after vesting. Value = full market price. No strike price. Different from stock options which require purchasing at strike price.
**Verified:** yes
