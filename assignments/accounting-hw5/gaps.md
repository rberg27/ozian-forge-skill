# Knowledge Gaps: Homework 5 — Stockholders' Equity and Costing Concepts

*Gaps identified during Socratic dialogue sessions.*

## Treasury Stock — Definition (Remember)
**Gap:** Initially described treasury stock as stock that stays with the company from incorporation — confused it with authorized-but-unissued shares.
**Teaching:** Treasury stock is shares that were previously issued and outstanding in the market, then repurchased by the company using cash. It sits in the equity section with a debit balance, reducing total equity — making it a contra-equity account.
**Verified:** yes

## Differential Analysis — Definition (Remember)
**Gap:** No prior knowledge of the term.
**Teaching:** Differential analysis compares only the costs and revenues that change depending on which alternative you choose. Costs and revenues that are identical under both options are irrelevant and excluded. Built from the insight that an income statement mixes avoidable and unavoidable costs together.
**Verified:** yes

## Fixed vs. Variable Costs — Direct vs. Indirect (Remember)
**Gap:** Knew variable and fixed cost definitions. Had direct vs. indirect reversed: said indirect costs disappear when a product is eliminated (wrong — those are direct/avoidable costs).
**Teaching:** Direct/avoidable fixed cost = disappears if the product is eliminated. Indirect/unavoidable fixed cost = persists regardless of whether the product exists. The test: would this cost survive if the product disappeared?
**Verified:** yes

## Contribution Margin — Definition (Remember)
**Gap:** Initially described contribution margin as margin requirements for options trading (unrelated). Didn't know it was selling price minus variable costs.
**Teaching:** Per-unit CM = selling price per unit − variable cost per unit. Total CM = total revenue − total variable costs. CM first covers fixed costs; whatever remains is profit.
**Verified:** yes

## RSU — Definition (Remember)
**Gap:** Described RSUs as the right to buy stock at a certain price — that's a stock option, not an RSU.
**Teaching:** An RSU is a promise to grant an employee shares for free after a vesting/service period. No purchase price. On vesting, the shares are simply delivered. Value = full market price on vesting date. Differs from a stock option, which requires buying at a strike price and is only valuable if market price > strike price.
**Verified:** yes
